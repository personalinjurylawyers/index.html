Every state has set up its online snooping laws to deal with cases and to support the investigatory powers against the citizen’s rights. The objective is to improve the transparency in the whole process and more importantly legal certainty.

Online surveillance laws typically aim to regulate online activity, more importantly, the role of social media is in focus under such practices. It’s a common discussion among common people that such laws and policies oppose the basic rights of privacy and free speech. But in reality, such laws tend to cover up both positive and negative sides of basic rights.

For instance, if we think about the recent data protection laws (GDPR), then you will see it attempts to protect citizens in term of information breach or theft by using illegal techniques or means. On the other side, this data protection also helps law enforcement agencies to take down, block, or remove websites or apps that are violating and breaching consumer privacy.

In all over the world, most countries even third world countries including India do also have rules in place to monitor and regulate the information passing over via the internet or other channels. However, each country deals with different aspects of complexities with unlike means. Therefore, one has to consult a professional before inducing any type of judgment or taking any decision.

In 2013, it goes public that the government of the USA and UK are collecting consumer data. Furthermore, the claims were that the USA is operating PRISM and UPSTREAM while UK is operating TEMPORA for data collection.

The aim of such programs is to anticipate the future needs and understanding of current conditions. The data collected under such programs is increasing its size by 10x every 5 years. Moreover, the mobile sensors data, social media pulses, and other user behavior are also growing.

Online surveillance laws or online snooping laws always initiate a debate in the community where some people are in the right of the bill while some are against it. But in all cases, the government is found contending for cybercrime, terrorism, data breach, and darknet risks and most importantly crimes associated with these activities.

As stated earlier most states have such regulations and laws in place and it’s not just the USA or UK. Such laws require the service providers to collect and intercept the data and store it separate for the potential probability of use in any upcoming crime investigation. Moreover, all businesses and consumers then have to follow the rules set by such regulations and especially business owners have to fulfill all mentioned requirements for smooth operations.
