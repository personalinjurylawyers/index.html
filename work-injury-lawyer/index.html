How to handle events after a workplace injury & before hiring a lawyer

  •  admin

Typically, all businesses are bound by statutory law to maintain workers compensation insurance to protect them against work injury or illness. Such insurance covers your medical expenses and offers some cash benefits to an employee in case of workplace injury. Sometimes work injury lawyer is required as the system can never fail proof, in such scenarios you may need representation to protect your rights.

What type of injuries falls under workplace injury?

Workplace injuries which fall under legally pursuable injuries include suffering from manual material handling, slips and falls, repetitive motion injuries and auto vehicle accidents. Further details for each type of scenario listed above are as below;

  • Repetitive motion injuries: Such workplace injuries which result from the repetitive movement for an extended period of time like carpal tunnel, bursitis and tendonitis are some conditions which are reported most commonly as a workplace injury.
  • Falls: Most commonly such type of cases are seen in the construction industry which leads to broken bones, traumatic brain injuries, spinal cord injuries and more. Typically, such injuries are due to the usage of defective equipment and unsafe work environment.
  • Manual material handling: This typically results in back injuries caused by handling, lifting, holding loads. Back injuries are the most common work-related injuries among people who manually handle materials.
  • Motor vehicle accidents: If an employee is doing some work-related task then vehicle accidents are compensable from the employer side. Moreover, employees will not be able to get compensation for injuries during coming to and going from the work location, except for some special scenarios.
  • Slips & falls: Typically, such cases are not covered by work injury law unless any hazardous condition is the cause of that accident. Such cases cover broken bones, head injuries, and other related injuries.

What one should do after injury & who is eligible for a claim

If you are facing a work injury then you must immediately report to your supervisor or floor manager. The requirements vary region wise, however, one must do it as soon as possible. Failure to report may prevent you from protecting your rights and hire a worker’s compensation lawyer.

Once you notify your employer, then you must receive the required medical treatment. Moreover, your employer will take care of insurance related work and claim the worker’s compensation for injuries.

One must fulfill some specific requirements to become eligible to claim workers’ compensation benefits.

  • Worker’s Comp Insurance: Your employer must carry workers’ compensation insurance. Statutory law requirements vary on the basis of a number of factors.
  • Your Employee status: You must have an employee status during the event of a work injury. No matter you fall under part-time, full-time, seasonal or temporary employees. Moreover, domestic workers, farm laborers and all other undocumented labor may not qualify for a work injury.
  • Job-related duties: If you are injured outside the premises but during the performance of job-related duties. Then you may not be able to get any compensation as such events fall under a gray area.

Your legal rights & what to look in work injury lawyer

Generally, workers’ compensation does not cover personal injury claims which an injured employee must be entitled to. Therefore, it’s really important that you know your rights in detail to fight back and protect your legal rights. In some scenarios, one can also file a lawsuit against the company to recover additional damages. Contacting and engaging with a work injury lawyer is a critical decision during any event of injury and can help you understand & protect your rights.

A best personal injury lawyer will be one who protects your rights in your best interest. To select a perfect match you should ask for their backgrounds and experience during the first meeting. Ask work injury lawyer about their assessment for your case, what will be their fees and charges? Are they willing to handle your case? All these questions will help you understand and look for a big picture.

Typically, people do not need to hire a workplace injury lawyer as the workers’ compensation system is working fine. However, sometimes still there may be a need for that, where one needs to engage with a lawyer to fight for his/her rights.
